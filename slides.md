# Meteoriks: what juroring actually looks like

<div class="subtitle">A practical inside view</div>

???

This is meant as a practical talk, not drama and not PR.

---

# Why make this talk?

- many people have opinions about Meteoriks
- fewer know what the work actually looks like
- even fewer think of joining it themselves

???

This is not "the official truth". It is an attempt to explain what participation feels like in practice.

---

# Why Meteoriks matters

- nominations give productions extra visibility
- awards influence what gets remembered
- the process matters enough that participation matters too

???

Do not oversell Meteoriks as the only measure of quality. It still has a real effect on visibility and memory.

---

# Misconception #1: recommendations are votes

<div class="columns">
  <div class="card">
    <h3>Common assumption</h3>
    <p>More recommendations = better chances</p>
  </div>
  <div class="card gold">
    <h3>What they actually do</h3>
    <p>Help a production get noticed</p>
    <p class="accent">They are not nomination points.</p>
  </div>
</div>

???

Recommendations are for discovery, not campaigning.

---

# Misconception #2: the workload

- yes, there can be a lot to review
- no, this does not mean everyone watches everything in full
- it depends on category, tools, experience and team size
- too few people makes the bad version of the workload much worse

???

Keep this in the middle: do not sugarcoat the work, but do not make it sound like torture either.

---

# What actually helps

- screenshots and captures
- following releases during the year
- category knowledge
- quick filtering of clearly irrelevant material
- more people sharing the work

---

# Why more jurors would help so much

- better load distribution
- fewer blind spots
- less last-minute recruiting
- more actual discussion
- better optics too

<p class="small">Not just more people: more active and competent people.</p>

---

# Who should consider doing it?

- people with real knowledge in some part of the scene
- people who actually watch productions
- people who can stay engaged over time
- people who can discuss things fairly
- people who care enough to do it properly

???

Not everybody should join. More of the right people should consider it.

---

# Outreach should travel better

- recruitment often happens in the same visible places
- the scene is wider than that
- different communities, language groups, parties, platforms and circles
- outreach should reflect that better

---

# Things that could improve

- clearer explanation of what recommendations do
- earlier recruitment
- wider outreach
- better communication inside juries
- perhaps more transparency around preselection

???

Keep this broad. This is not a complete reform programme.

---

# What I hope this talk leads to

- better understanding of the process
- fewer lazy myths
- more qualified people considering juroring
- more communities feeling invited in
