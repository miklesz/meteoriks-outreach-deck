# Meteoriks presentation outline

Draft for review by Meteoriks.
Reusable version.
Not final slides.

General assumption: this should be a simple outreach talk that explains the process a bit, clears up a few myths, and maybe convinces some more people to get involved.

Not trying to be:

- a full process documentation
- a "how the sausage is made" expose
- a rant about results
- an official Meteoriks manifesto

## Slide 6

**Title:** `What the experience depends on`

**On slide:**

Could be 2 columns.

Left:

- `head of jury`
- `how they organize the work`
- `how they move discussion forward`

Right:

- `other jurors`
- `how active they are`
- `how engaged they are in discussion`
- `how much overlap there is in what people watched`

Maybe one short line at the bottom:

`same general process, very different experience depending on people`

**Graphic:**

- no separate graphic needed
- or just a simple 2-column layout

**Speaker note:**

This is probably worth saying explicitly: participation does not feel identical from jury to jury. A lot depends on who is leading it and how engaged the rest of the group is.

**On slide:**

## Slide 8

**Title:** `Misconception #2: the workload`

**On slide:**

- `yes, there can be a lot to review`
- `no, this does not mean everyone watches everything in full`
- `it depends on category, tools, experience, team size`
- `too few people makes the bad version of the workload much worse`

**Graphic:**

- optional
- maybe 4 boxes: `category/tools/experience/team size`

**Speaker note:**

This should land somewhere in the middle: not sugarcoating it, not making it sound like torture either.
